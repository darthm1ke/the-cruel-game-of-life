The binge mechanic should be one of the central threats, because it turns “hunger” from a simple red bar into a loaded gun. The player thinks they are doing well by starving, but they are actually charging up a disaster.

A good brutal loop could be:

Daily Cycle

Morning starts with weight, hunger, energy, mood, money, debt, and health risk.
Player chooses actions: work, walk, cook, skip meal, cheap snack, search online, sleep.
Hunger rises if calories are too low.
Energy drops if food quality is bad, sleep is bad, depression is high, or weight is too high.
If hunger + depression + craving crosses the binge threshold, the day is lost.

Binge Event
When triggered:

The player loses control for the rest of the day.
Income/savings are eaten away by a random percentage.
Weight jumps brutally, maybe +3% to +10% of recent loss or a flat weight spike.
Mood crashes.
Shame/depression rises.
Next day’s binge threshold becomes easier to trigger.

That is nasty, but mechanically beautiful.

The energy mechanic should punish the obvious “just eat more” solution too:

Cheap food reduces hunger but gives poor energy.
Overeating reduces movement and work ability.
High sugar / ultra-cheap meals can cause a health crash.
Health crash can mean hospital days, debt, lost work, depression, and maybe permanent stat damage.
Surviving can be worse than dying because the debt makes the next cycle harder.

So the player gets trapped between:

Starve: save money, lose weight fast, risk binge/game over.
Eat cheap: reduce hunger, gain weight, lose energy, risk health crash.
Eat healthy: best option, but expensive.
Work: earn money, but costs energy.
Exercise: helps slightly, but costs energy and hunger.
Miracle treatment: lowers hunger, but expensive/risky/sketchy.
Rest: recovers energy, but loses income and progress.

The brutal part is that every “solution” creates a new problem. That is the game.

I would add one very cruel stat: Control.

Control is not willpower exactly. It is the player’s ability to make choices. It gets damaged by hunger, depression, debt, poor sleep, cravings, and shame. When Control hits zero, the game starts choosing for you: binge, sleep all day, miss work, doomscroll, order food, ignore bills.

That would make the player feel the vicious cycle instead of just reading about it.

For tone, I would keep the language darkly funny but not cartoonishly cruel. Example events:

“You watched three meal-prep videos. No food was prepared.”
“Your body has filed a complaint against management.”
“A cheap snack restored hope for 11 minutes.”
“The scale has chosen violence.”
“You survived the hospital. The invoice did not.”

This has real potential because it is brutally unfair in a way that mirrors the subject. The difficulty should feel like the point: not impossible because the game is badly balanced, but because every system is quietly conspiring against the player.
